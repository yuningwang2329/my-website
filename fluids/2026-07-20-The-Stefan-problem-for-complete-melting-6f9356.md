# The Stefan problem for complete melting of finitely strained solids into viscoelastic fluids

- **作者 (Authors)**: Tomáš Roubíček
- **来源 (Source)**: Arxiv (math.AP)
- **日期 (Date)**: 2026-07-20
- **原文链接 (Link)**: [查看原始论文](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.18547v1)

## 中文摘要

具有热机械相变的可压缩流固相互作用 (FSI) 在欧拉框架内的大应变下被公式化。对于偏流部分，采用具有附加粘度的杰弗里斯（也称为反齐纳）流变学。控制机械固液转变的核心原理是粘性（或粘塑性）响应与温度相关，并且在解冻过程中可能完全退化为粘弹性流体，因此剪切变形不存在弹性响应。这种行为使得流体能够自由流动，随后冻结成新的配置，并有可能重新熔化回流体，从而允许这样的循环无限期地重复。经典的 Stefan 问题与一级（融化-冷冻）相变的潜热相关，通过结合动力学过热和过冷而得到增强。利用多极非简单连续体的概念，通过适当截断的时间离散化分析应用于原始公式的更高梯度修改。

## 英文摘要

The compressible fluid-solid interaction (FSI) with a thermomechanical phase transition is formulated at large strains within the Eulerian frame. For the deviatoric part, the Jeffreys (also called anti-Zener) rheology with an additional viscosity is adopted. The core philosophy governing the mechanical solid-liquid transition is that the viscous (or viscoplastic) response is temperature-dependent and may fully degenerate to a viscoelastic fluid during thawing, so that there is no elastic response on the shear distortion. This behavior enables the free flow of the fluid, its subsequent freezing into a new configuration, and potential re-melting back into a fluid, allowing such cycles to repeat indefinitely. The classical Stefan problem, associated with the latent heat of the first-order (thawing-freezing) phase transition, is augmented by incorporating kinetic overheating and undercooling. The analysis by a time discretization with an appropriate truncation is applied to a higher-gradient modification of the original formulation, utilizing the concept of multipolar nonsimple continua.
