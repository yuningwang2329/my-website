# Inertial motion of incompressible continua

- **作者 (Authors)**: Francesca Berlinghieri, Giulio G. Giusteri
- **来源 (Source)**: Arxiv (math.AP)
- **日期 (Date)**: 2026-07-17
- **原文链接 (Link)**: [查看原始论文](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.16043v1)

## 中文摘要

我们在几何和变分框架内研究不可压缩连续体的惯性运动，将经典的 Arnold-Ebin-Marsden 理论从固定域的体积保持微分同胚组扩展到具有可变图像的变形配置空间。在后一种情况下，缺乏群体结构需要证明一些在经典环境中直接出现的结果。我们证明，具有适当规律性的方向保持变形构成了希尔伯特流形，并且体积保持变形形成了具有切向量的子流形，该子流形通过拉格朗日到欧拉图片对应关系映射到无散度向量场。在此过程中，我们还提出了与可压缩连续体相对应的几何结构。连续体的动能产生拉格朗日作用（从中推导出惯性运动方程）和度量（具有通过惯性运动精确识别的测地线）。虽然惯性运动只能与不可压缩完美流体的物理运动一致，但它可以用于为通用连续体提供配置流形的自然参数化。我们得到了测地流方程解的局部时间存在性的一般结果，并且给出了明确的例子，表明对于相同的初始数据，可压缩情况下连续体后面的测地线可以在有限时间内留下允许变形的流形，而相应的不可压缩测地线则在所有时间都存在。

## 英文摘要

We study the inertial motion of incompressible continua within a geometric and variational framework, extending the classical Arnold-Ebin-Marsden theory from the group of volume-preserving diffeomorphisms of a fixed domain to configuration spaces of deformations with variable image. In the latter case, the lack of a group structure requires proving some results that are instead immediate in the classical setting. We show that the orientation-preserving deformations with suitable regularity constitute a Hilbert manifold and that volume-preserving deformations form a submanifold with tangent vectors that are mapped onto divergence-free vector fields by the Lagrangian-to-Eulerian-picture correspondence. In so doing, we also present the geometric structure corresponding to compressible continua. The kinetic energy of the continuum gives rise to both a Lagrangian action, from which the equations of inertial motion are deduced, and a metric, with geodesics that are identified precisely by inertial motions. While the inertial motion can coincide with a physical one only for incompressible perfect fluids, it can be used to provide a natural parametrization of the configuration manifold for generic continua. We obtain a general result of local-in-time existence of solutions for the geodesic flow equation and we present explicit examples showing that, for the same initial data, the geodesic followed by the continuum in the compressible case can leave the manifold of admissible deformations in finite time, while the corresponding incompressible geodesic exists for all times.
