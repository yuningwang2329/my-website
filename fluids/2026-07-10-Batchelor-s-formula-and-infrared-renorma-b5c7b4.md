# Batchelor's formula and infrared renormalization for sedimentation

- **作者 (Authors)**: Mitia Duerinckx, Antoine Gloria
- **来源 (Source)**: Arxiv (math.AP)
- **日期 (Date)**: 2026-07-10
- **原文链接 (Link)**: [查看原始论文](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.09995v1)

## 中文摘要

我们研究斯托克斯流中刚性颗粒的静态随机悬浮液的沉降。巴彻勒公式预测了由于悬浮颗粒之间的流体动力学相互作用而对无限体积平均沉降速度进行的第一次稀释修正。严格的推导长期以来一直受到斯托克斯流的长程性质的阻碍，斯托克斯流会在大体积极限下产生红外发散。在维度 $d>2$ 中，对于满足定量去相关假设的静止悬浮液，我们构建了无限体积平均沉降速度，并表明它控制着大型容器中颗粒的相对沉降速度，与容器形状无关。然后，我们在稀释状态下建立了该平均沉降速度的重整化簇展开，并将其计算到双粒子项，从而证明了巴彻勒公式的合理性。该证明基于流体动力相互作用的红外重整化。无限体积可观测量被分解为显式奇异部分，带有不可积的大规模贡献，以及由椭圆估计控制的常规余数。奇异部分通过对悬浮液产生的发散平均回流进行编码的反项进行重新归一化。在稀簇展开的层面上，重整化是逐簇实现的，奇异正则分解是通过流体动力相互作用的有限图解展开来实现的，其灵感来自反射方法，隔离了主要发散的子结构并暴露了关键的抵消。

## 英文摘要

We study the sedimentation of stationary random suspensions of rigid particles in Stokes flow. Batchelor's formula predicts the first dilute correction to the infinite-volume mean settling speed due to hydrodynamic interactions between suspended particles. A rigorous derivation has long been obstructed by the long-range nature of the Stokes flow, which gives rise to infrared divergences in the large-volume limit. In dimension $d>2$, for stationary suspensions satisfying quantitative decorrelation assumptions, we construct the infinite-volume mean settling speed and show that it governs the relative settling speed of particles in large containers, independently of the container shape. We then establish a renormalized cluster expansion of this mean settling speed in the dilute regime and compute it up to the two-particle term, thereby justifying Batchelor's formula. The proof is based on the infrared renormalization of hydrodynamic interactions. Infinite-volume observables are decomposed into an explicit singular part, carrying the non-integrable large-scale contribution, and a regular remainder controlled by elliptic estimates. The singular part is renormalized through counterterms that encode the diverging mean backflow generated by the suspension. At the level of the dilute cluster expansion, the renormalization is implemented cluster by cluster and the singular-regular decomposition is achieved through a finitary diagrammatic expansion of hydrodynamic interactions, inspired by the method of reflections, which isolates the leading divergent substructures and exposes the key cancellations.
